Morning · Intention
How do you want to show up today?
Not what you want to get done — how you want to feel and move through the day. One sentence is enough.
```
Examples
"I want to stay grounded and not get pulled into reactive mode at work." · "I want to make real progress on the business without guilt about the day job." · "I want today to feel intentional, not like I just got through it."
```
Office day · Prepare
Anything you need to know or handle before the day starts?
Think through the day now so you're not caught off guard later.
```
I know what meetings or calls are happening today
Any prep needed for those meetings is done or planned
I know what deal work or deliverables are due or moving today
Bag packed, outfit ready, commute is accounted for
I've noted any A Worthy Partner tasks that need to happen outside office hours today
```
WFH day · Prepare
WFH days have more freedom — and more drift risk.
Think through the boundary between job work and business work before the day starts.
```
I know what Big Four work has to happen today
I know when I'm switching from job mode to business mode today
Any meetings or calls are on my radar and prepped
My workspace is set up to support focus — not just existing in it
The WFH drift trap
Home days can feel like they belong to the business before the job is actually done. Name your cutover point now so neither bleeds into the other.
```
Weekend · Prepare
What does today actually need to hold?
Weekends are personal and open — but they still need a shape or they float. Think through the day now.
```
I know which Saturday mode I'm in (Mode A or Mode B) or what Sunday's sequence is
My business task for today is named and I know what I'm sitting down to
I know what maintenance or errands need to happen today
I've been outside or I'm going outside before anything else starts
Weekend drift
The float happens when the day has no first move. What's the first thing you're doing after this check-in? Name it before you close this.
```
